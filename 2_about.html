My Coding History

My name is Edward Shin. I am a designer, a programmer, and an amateur writer.
Computers was the beginning of my long journey. My first love for coding
started with my love for games. I took a game development course at the
age of 12 with Game Maker 5. After learning the basics, I explored the
software more and created more complex games.

During my second year of high school, I studied computer science with Java. I
was drilled in all the basics of the programming language and how to solve
problems. Towards the end of my senior year, I came across a renowned game
called "Cave Story" created by Software Engineer Daisuke "Pixel" Amaya. I
immediately fell in love with its plotline and its retro-like style, a style
that I had been denied because of my parents' refusal to allow me to own a
game console at a young age.

Once I got into Carnegie Mellon University, I started studying in the School of
Architecture - as I thought computer science was overwhelming during high
school. During my second semester of my first year of college, however, I was
introduced to Processing, a programming platform for artists. My love for this
software not only rekindled my passion for programming, but it also led me to
pursue Carnegie Mellon's IDeaTe program, where students can learn about
combining technology with art. I studied media and game design, and took a few
courses from the Computer Science department. I hope to graduate and continue
to learn more about computers and art combined.

Skills

I know JavaScript, HTML5, CSS3, Python, Processing 2, and p5.js.

Other Interests

Aside from programming, I love writing stories, constructing models, and drawing
figures and characters
