StandPi: The Portable Standing Desk

This is my newest portable standing desk for laptops. Using the existing StandStand as a base, I made the desk with fewer materials to lighten the weight and cost without sacrifing its strength and stability. Unlike The table also has a series of holes cut out to allow the laptop to ventilate when it overheats. At the same time, it will still allow other objects to be placed on top of the desk.

Tools: Laser Cutting, Rhino

Materials: 1/4" Acrylic (clear), Water-Resistant Paper

Portable Laptop Stand (Desk)

This is my custom-designed portable laptop stand. This is designed for ease on body, portability, and cost-efficiency. Not only can this be compressed into a 9" by 9" by 1/2" object, it also comes in two elevated heights at roughly 6" and 9". This makes for an excellent substitute for expensive and stationary computer desks.

Tools: Laser Cutting, Solidworks, Inkscape

Materials: 1/8" and 1/4" Acrylic (clear, colored)

Totem Signs (Puzzle)

Totem Signs is a 3D printed puzzle. Each disc can be turned to change the arrangement of signs on each column. The goal is to turn the discs until all the columns do not have a repeating shape. Upon solving it, the discs can be removed and rearranged to give players a fresh challenge.

Tools: Zortrax M200, Laser Cutter

Materials: ABS Plastic, 1/8" Acrylic (colored)

Portable Phone Stand

This is my custom-designed phone stand. This is designed for smart phone users to allow them to set their phones in a standing position for ease of use. It can be disassembled into two pieces and bundled into a single 2" by 5" by 3/8" unit for portablility. This stand can support just about any smart phone in portrait or landscape positions.

Tools: 3D Printing, Solidworks, Rhinoceros, Inkscape

Materials: ABS Plastic

Portable Laptop Stand (Standing)

This is my original design for a standing laptop stand. This is designed for ease on body, portability, and cost-efficiency. Each of the two pieces are rougly 10" by 11" by 0.25", allowing for easy transportation. What makes this stand unique from other stnads is that it takes one acrylic rectangle and splits it into two sturdy parts instead of using two or more separate ones, allowing the product to be made with less material and cost. This makes for an excellent substitute for expensive and stationary standing computer desks.

Tools: Laser Cutting, Solidworks, Rhinoceros, Inkscape

Materials: 1/4" Acrylic (clear)

Marble Climber

This is a replication of an existing marble climber. Fascinated by the idea of an automaton moving a detached object, I created a series of Solidworks parts and printed a 3D printed version, which the marble climber lacks as it is commonly made of wood.

Tools: Laser Cutting, 3D printer, Solidworks

Materials: ABS plastic

Collapsable Rubberband Gun

This is my original collapsable rubber band gun. Inspired by the James Bond movie, "The Man With the Golden Gun", I wanted to make a rubber band gun that can be disguised as something else. In "Stealth Mode", it measures about 3.5" by 8" by 0.75". In "Firing Mode", the length is extended from 8" to 14". The gun can fire up to 9 rubber bands, and pieces interlock in Stealth mode, so transportation is smooth.

Tools: Laser Cutting, Solidworks, Rhinoceros, Inkscape

Materials: 1/8" and 1/4" Acrylic (black)
