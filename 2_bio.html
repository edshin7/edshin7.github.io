About Me

Biography


Hello. My name is Edward Shin. I am a designer and a programmer. Computers was the beginning of my long journey. My first love for coding started with my love for games. I took a game development course at the age of 12 with Game Maker 5. After learning the basics, I explored the software more and created more complex games.

During my second year of high school, I studied computer science with Java. I was drilled in all the basics of the programming language and how to solve problems. Towards the end of my senior year, I came across a renowned computer game called "Cave Story" created by Software Engineer Daisuke "Pixel" Amaya. I immediately fell in love with its plotline and its retro-style art -- a style that gaves a strong sense of love and nostalgia for older video games.

Following my passion for the arts and buildings, I joined Carnegie Mellon University's School of Architecture. During the second semester of my first year of college, however, I was introduced to Processing, a programming platform for artists. My love for this software not only rekindled my passion for programming, but it also led me to pursue Carnegie Mellon's IDeaTe program, where students can learn about combining technology with art. I studied media and game design, and took a few courses from the Computer Science department. Along with my major in Architecture, I aim to graduate with a minor in Media Design and to pursue a career that merges technology with art.
